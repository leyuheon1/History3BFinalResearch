In the Iberian peninsula, Spain has finally driven out the last remnant of the Muslim forces from Granada in the year 1492. The Reconquista was finally accomplished. The two monarchs, Ferdinand of Aragon and Isabella of Castile, are very important because not only they united the whole Iberian peninsula but also they sponsored Columbus's voyage. Moreover, their daughter, Joanna of Castile, married the Habsburg prince, Philip I of Habsburg. This led to the golden age of the Habsburg dynasty when the Habsburg family ruled Spain, Austria, Hungary, significant part of Italy, and Netherlands at the same time. But for now, we focus on the Columbus's voyage to the Americas.source1
Columbus's "discovery of the New World" is a significant moment in the human history. First of all, a lot of new foods like potatoes and red pepper from the Americas were imported to the Eurasian continent. Korean Kimchi would have looked very different without the red pepper from the Americas. source2
Also, Columbus's discovery of the New World brought in a lot of silver from the New World which was then exported to Ming China. Ming China used paper currency but because of excess issuance of paper monies, the currency became almost useless. Thus, people started using silver as the main currency for business and slowly, the government adopted silver coins as well. So, Ming China was in dire need of silver and Spain was drowned in silver. Silver started to flow from Americas into Spain and then to China while the manufactured goods from China flowed to Spain. The naval trade between Spain and Ming China can be considered as the early form of globalization.source3

But Columbus and his successors had commited horrible atrocities. They enslaved thousands of natives forcing them to work in the silver mines with terrible conditions. Also, the conquistadors brought in numerous diseases from the Old World which killed millions of Native Americans. Finally, after the labor became scarce due to disease and massacres, Columbus and his successors brought slaves from Africa. This is why we should learn about the Columbus's voyage and its signficance in history but not consider him as some kind of a hero.
