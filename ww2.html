The peace treaty at Versailles laid heavy burden on Germany. Although not much territorial changes had been made compared to other Central Powers, Germany had to pay a huge sum of money as a reparations. Also, there was resentment prevalent among Germans of the abrupt surrender. It was hard for Germans to accept the reality that their proud country had to surrender when they controlled vast amount of lands stretching from Belgium to Ukraine. The reparations, which were worth $296 billion today, fueled the German anger against the Allies. On top of that the economic crisis during the interwar period destabilized German politics and increased the popularity of Nazis. With Nazis controlling the German politics, Germany rapidly rearmed itself and went back to an aggressive expansionist policies.
Another problem was brewing in the East. Japanese empire had beccome an industrial giant and its industrial production had already surpassed that of France. But Japan's industry heavily depended on imports from regions it had conquered or from foreign trades. Japanese steel industry required large amount of pig iron and coal from China. Japan also had to import almost all of its pertoleum fuels. With the increase of protectionist policies around the globe where each superpower was building its own bloc of economy, Japanese elites were getting concerned of the country's lack of natural resources. Furthermore, the military was getting more and more poltical powers. The military was spending 31 percent of the government spending in 1931-1932 and 47 percent of the government spending in 1936-1937. When the finance minstry under Takahashi tried to stop this trend, he was assassinated by the militarists. Finally, the Japanese military thought that the reason that China could fight against Japan for so long is because of the economic support of the West which came through Burma Road and French Indochina. The Japanese Navy and eventually the Army concluded that the clash with Britain, France, and America was inevitable.
German aggression started with the remilitarization of the Rhineland on March, 7, 1936. Today, people wonder why the democratic nations in the West(UK, France, and USA) did not step into stop Hitler. First, Britains, French and Americans were tired of the World War I and wanted to prevent next major war at all cost. But more importantly, these democratic nations could not coordinate their policies to contain German aggression becuase of the internal turmoils in the interwar period. US economy had been hit hard by the Great Depression. Britain and France were disunited and did not like each other's approach to Germany and Italy and Hitler took full advantage of this situation. After the remilitarization of the Rhineland, Nazi Germany annexed Austria on March 13, 1938. Then he went on to annex Sudentland, the German speaking region of Czechoslovakia on September 30, 1938. Eventually, Hitler annexed rest of Czechoslovakia in March, 1939. Then, the Nazi Germnay and the Soviet Union signed Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact, the non-aggression pact between the two countries on August 23, 1939. Finally, Hitler invaded Poland, on September 1, 1939.
German generals had far better understanding of the modern technologies and how to utilize them in the battlefield compared to their French counterparts. While the French generals believed that the superior firepower is the key to victory, German generals realized that speed is the key to the modern warfare and that the tank divisons should be utilized independently from the infantry divisions to maximize its speed. The key to this new warfare or Blitzkreig was the speed and concentration. Although only 15 percent of the Germany army was mechanized, German divisions could numerically outnumber their enemies thanks to the concentration of their forces at the weakest part of the enemey line. Flexibility of German decision making system was also vital in Blitzkreig. German army employed mission-type tactics where the emphasis is on the outcome of a mission rather than the specific ways of achieving it. So if the commander figures out that alternative route of attack is preferrable, he or she could order the troop to attack through different route without having to go through the chain of command. Thus, German military could exploit the weakness in enemy line better than the French. Finally, the use of modern technologies, like radios and airplanes helped Germans a lot. Germany used dive bombers as fast moving artilleries which could support the panzers. Also, while the French relied most of its communication through telephone lines, German troops used radios for communication. Thus, when the Panzer divisions made a breakthrough in French lines, encircle the French army and cut the telephone lines in the rear, French troops panicked. With the use of this new tactics, German troops could overwhelm their French counterparts and France fell on June, 1940.
But because of the body of water that is blocking Great Britain from German Blitzkreig, Germany could not deploy the same tatic against Great Britain. Slowly the war was changing into the war of attrition. Germany's decision to attack the Soviet Union amplified the problem as USSR had much superior industrial capacity compared to Nazi Germany. Also, the Red Army adopted the German tactics and was deploying much more tanks compared to the Wehrmacht. Finally, the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor dragged US into the war and sealed the fate of the Facists.
