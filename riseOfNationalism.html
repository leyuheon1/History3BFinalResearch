Am Anfang war Napoleon(In the beginning was Napoleon.)
Thomas Nipperdy starts his famous book about the German history with this line. One of the lasting legacies of Napoleon was the rise of German nationalism. Before the Napoleonic Wars, "Germany" was divided into hundreds of independent states sharing little sense of national identity.

But Napoleon reorganized these German states into 39 larger states and established the Confederation of the Rhine which were comprised of 16 German states. Moreover, the German elites realized that they cannot protect themselves from foreign powers if they did not support each other. So the German princes encouraged nationalistic feelings among the German population. source1

After the Congress of Vienna in 1815, German states formed the German Confederation which seek to protect its member states. Furthermore, as the industrial revolution gained pace, German entrepreneurs asked to abolish the tarrif barriers to maximize their profits. Thus, the Zollverin or the Customs Union was formed under the Prussian leadership in 1834. This further stimulated the sense of unity among German nations. source2 source3


The rise of nationalism was not restricted to German states. Pan-Slavism was rising in the eastern and east central Europe where Austria-Hungarian empire and Ottoman empire used to rule. Pan-Slavic movement further eroded the already waning Austrian-Hungarian empire and the Ottoman empire. Also the Pan-Slavic movement encouraged Russia to form a close relationship with Slavic nations such as Serbia and this was one of the reason why World War I became the "World War" instead of a locally contained military conflict. source4
